Dr.Tsien Hsue-Shen

Father of Chinese Rocketry

Tsien Hsue-Shen is at the center in this Octerber,1954 photo, taken in front of Caltech's Franklin Thomas Laboratory of Engineering.

Biography of Dr.Tsien Hsue-Shen

Early life and education

  • 1911 - born in Hangzhou, Qing dynasty China.
  • 1934 - recieved a degree in mechanical engineering from Chiao Tung University (now spelled JiaoTong) in Shanghai.
  • 1936 - received Master of Science degree from MIT.
  • 1939 - earned doctorate form Caltech with a thisis on slender body theory at high speeds.

Caree in the United States

  • 1943 - drafted the first document to use the name Jet Propulsion Laboratory.
  • 1947 - take up a teaching position at MIT.
  • 1949 - became the first director of the Daniel and Florence Guggenheim Jet Propulsion Center at Caltech.

Detention

  • 1950 - announced would be resigning from Caltech and returning to China.
  • 1950 - detained at Terminal Island for two weeks.
  • 1951 - Tsien was declared subject and forbidden from leaving Los Angeles Country without permisson

Return to China

  • 1955 - Tsien departed from Los Angeles aboard the Grover Cleveland.
  • 1956 - leaded the program of China's Dongfeng ballistic missiles and the Long March space rockets.

Later life

  • 1980s - studied in a number of areas, and created systematics.
  • 1991 - retired and lived quitely in Beijing.
  • 2008 - named Aviation Week and Space Technology person of the year.
  • 2009 - the Omega Alpha Association named Tsien one of four Honoray Member in the international sysytems engineering honor society.
  • 2009 - died in Beijing at age 97.
